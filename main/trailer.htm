Alan's Cycle Trailers Page
Page updated Mar 13
I am posting here info on the cycle trailers I have made in the hope they will help/encourage others to have a go at making their own. They are very easy to make and very useful. The first trailer is designed to be handy for light work (general grocery shopping etc.) where as the second trailer is designed for much more heavy work.


Trailer 1 - light shopping trailer/trolley


See this trailer on my latest bike HERE

I have a couple of cycle trailers but these are not ideal for shopping as it can be difficult to find a place to park and if you do there is a good chance someone will be stolen whilst you are in the shop, so I decided to see if I can make a trailer which would be more suited.

This trailer quickly detaches from the cycle and can then be wheeled into the shop (as a standard shopping trolley), it then clips onto the supermarkets shopping trolley whilst doing my shopping, at the checkout I load the items straight into my trailer and then wheel it to my cycle and clip it back on - works great :-)

The trailer links to my bike via a modified quick release compressed air hose attachment, this allows the fitting to twist freely and the other free movement required by a cycle trailer hitch is simply provided by a short length of rubber hose (water hose as used in a car) - The wheels were purchased from eBay for under £15

BTW - I have made the wheels easy to remove for storage (and easier carrying in a car boot etc.)

If you don't fancy making your own, it turns out there is something similar available commercially HERE or HERE


Notes:

With it being pretty top heavy I wouldn't try taking any corners at great speed with one of these, but for popping to the local shops it is ideal

If I was building it again I think I would make the frame from something lighter than the wood I used and maybe more support around the handle as there can be a lot of stress in that area when braking.
I suspect there must be a better way to attach the tow bar to the trolley?
Having said that, I have carried a 56lb weight in mine (when testing it) no problem and when empty you pretty much wouldn't know it was there.

The wheels I used for this project is a kids cycle wheel - lots on eBay HERE


As I was so impressed with this trailer I have paid out for some better wheels (they are kids cycle wheels from eBay for £10 each)

Trailer 2 - Heavy Duty Trailer

This trailer I made very big and strong for when I wish to move more heavy or large items.  I have even used this to move a washing machine - whilst I wouldn't like to try and do this a great distance it is no problem towing such an item and this trailer has proved very useful on many occasions.
The linkage to the bike is more complex than the above trailer as it has to be more solid but all I use on the cycle is a large bolt with an eye on it and so this allows the linkage to turn freely in two directions, the linkage on the trailer then provides the free movement in the third.
