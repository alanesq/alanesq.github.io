Alan's Schickard Calculator Page

My home made Schickard Calculator

As with most of my projects, it was a sudden and ill thought through decision to do it and rather than study or plan first what I intend to do I just jump in and make a start, so this is not historically correct etc. but is loosly based on information kindly provided by Jens Puhle HERE and adapted to be easy/cheap for me to build using materials easily available (mostly MDF) and using my home made CNC router.

I first learned of the existence of the Schickard Calculator on 10th November and on 29th I had finished building my own which has to be some kind of record? ;-)

My collection of old mechanical calculators





I found the imprtant thing was to get the shape of the indexing arms correct, once I had done this the carry works surprisingly well although if it is carrying across several wheels it can require some assistance which I believe is normal for these machines judging from other videos I have seen. This actually works well though as you can easily see which wheels have not fully turned and simply give them a tweak to help them to the next digit.
If you request by email I can send you the drawings I used to create it on my CNC, they are just simple Inkscape files which I then used JScut to produce the gcode. I used brass welding rods for axles and the inspiration I was most pleased with was to use the inserts from electrical block connectors to attach the gear wheels to the axle.
