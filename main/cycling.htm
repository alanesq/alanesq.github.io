Compulsary use of cycle paths

Reasons cyclists may choose not to use cycle paths:
1 - It is not really very suitable for cycling as it is narrow and shared with pedestrians
2 - If you do use it you will very likely get abuse from pedestrians and told to get on the road
3 - Many people cycle at well over 20mph and to do so on a cycle path would be very dangerous
4 - if cycling a long distance (especially if you do not know the area well) it can be a very slow and confusing journey trying to figure out which cycle path is suitable or where they actually go and having to deal with a junction at every side street.
5 - Some people feel they are being forced off the roads (where they have a right to be) and worry they WILL one day be forced off the roads if they do not use them.
6 - Cycle paths invariably involve crossing many junctions which could be argued (especially if you cycle at a reasonable speed) are a greater risk.
7 - I would say most cycle paths are simply not fit for purpose. I actually enjoy using them as they are an entertaining challenge to use and offer many hilarious stupidities. but in general they are mostly a bit of a joke in my opinion.
8 - The cyclist may feel it unfair to transfer the risk the car poses to the cyclist on to the pedestrian by sharing a path with them instead of facing the traffic.
9 - You tend to get more punctures on cycle paths
10 - Cycle paths are usually not gritted or cleared in the winter
11 - It is often unclear where cycle paths end so you risk accidentally riding on a footpath and being fined.

Highway Code: 61 - Cycle Routes and Other Facilities - "Use of these facilities is not compulsory and will depend on your experience and skills"

Jumping red lights

Whenever I go through traffic lights which are about to change you can almost guarantee one or even two cars will follow me through (i.e. despite the lights being red for them).
When waiting to cross a pedestrian crossing you will often see a car sail past when the lights have changed to red.
if it wasn't a common thing for cars to jump red lights then there would not be any red light cameras so why do people seem to fixate on cyclists jumping red lights as if they alone do it?
