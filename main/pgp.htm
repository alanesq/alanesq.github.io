Alan's Privacy Page

I request that people encrypt all emails to me if possible as most people use G-mail or similar free email services which means all emails sent or received are read and probably stored by Google (amongst others).  The amount of information Google has about us is terrifying and so I do not wish to give away my private information (or at least I expect to have the option and be payed for it if I agree). 
If you do not encrypt emails to me can you please avoid including any personal information etc.

This may sound a bit paranoid but just consider for a moment that assuming you have an Android phone and log in to Google on any computer you use, then Google know your exact location 24hrs a day, everything you put in your diary, everything you search for, everything you watch on Youtube, who you communicate with and what about, what your friends say to others about you etc. etc..  Would you be happy if everyone had this information about you?
i.e. If you send someone a letter in the post you expect it to be secure and no one to have opened and read it before the recipient receives it, if Royal Mail announced that they are going to read all letters before delivering them and sell any information they get from doing this people would rightly get upset, but with email people seem happy for this to happen?

I use PGP for encryption.  My public key can be found HERE


For a quick and easy method to send me an encrypted message you can use THIS program.  Just import my public key in to it, then copy and past the resulting encrypted text in to your usual email program.


On my Android phone I use K9-mail along with Openkeychain (Both can be downloaded from the Google app store but better to get them from F-droid)

On Linux I use Thunderbird with an Add-on called Enigmail

On Windows I believe you can add the ability to Outlook but it is not something I have done myself

btw - if you need to send a one off secure email to someone Proton-mail is worth a look
